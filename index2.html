How to Prepare Tamarind Chutney


In this recipe, you will learn how to make tamarind chutney.


  1. Soak 50g of tamarind pulp from the block tamarind in 150ml hot water to extract the juice or paste for 10-15 minutes (skip this step if using tamarind concentrate or paste).
  2. After steeping in hot water, pass the tamarind through a sieve to remove any fibres or seeds to obtain a smooth tamarind purée. Once you're done, you should have around 1/2 cup of tamarind purée.
  3. Coarsely grind the cumin seeds in a mortar and pestle and set them aside.
  4. Prepare the spice blend by coarsely grinding the fennel seeds in a mortar and pestle. To the fennel seeds add the dried ginger powder, dried mango powder, kashmiri chilli powder, black salt/regular salt and a pinch of garam masala.
  5. Heat a saucepan on medium heat and add the oil. Once the oil is hot, add the asafoetida and the crushed cumin seeds.
  6. Immediately add the jaggery, sugar, tamarind paste, spice blend and 1/4-1/2 cup of water.
  7. Bring the chutney to a simmer till it reaches a thick and saucy consistency.
  8. You can bottle the chutney in glass jars and store it in the fridge for over a month.
  9. Serve the chutney cold or at room temperature. Enjoy the chutney with snacks like pakoras, samosas or use it to make chaat.
